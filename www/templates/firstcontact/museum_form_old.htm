Important

The information you supply here is used to create the online listing for your museum on museums4schools. Filling in the basic facts about your museum and its services should be fairly straightforward. The survey also includes questions about the areas of the curriculum that your permanent displays, object loan or outreach service could support (for brevity these are all called “permanent displays” in the survey). This is so that teachers can search the online database by curriculum theme. They will want to know if there is plenty to see and do related to their area of study, so please only tick the themes for which your displays/object loan/outreach service really could be useful.

To keep updating to a minimum, the database does not include rapidly changing information. Rather, it will encourage teachers to get in touch with your museum or service directly to get up to date information on events, charging details, etc.

The curriculum themes are different for each Key Stage. The ages of children in each Key Stage are shown on the survey. If you know that school groups use your museum for other themes, please include these on the last page of the survey so that this information can be included in the listing for your museum.

For now, the online database is for primary teachers only. It will be extended to secondary teachers later in 2005, at which time you will be able to add information for Key Stages 3 and 4.

You don’t need to complete the survey all in one go. You can click “save and return later” at any time. The information will not appear on the website until you have finished the whole survey and it has been checked through by a member of our editorial staff.

Please give an overview of your museum or heritage organisation. This should be a snapshot (around 200 words), written for a teacher who has never heard of your museum or service.

The opening paragraph is the first bit teachers will read, so you may need to give it some thought. Describe what your organisation is and what it has to offer schools in a nutshell. Following paragraphs should give more detailed information, focussing on what you can offer to schools.
